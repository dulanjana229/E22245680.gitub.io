Ingredients:

    For the Pasta:

    • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour
    • 2 large eggs
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • Pinch of salt

    For the sauce:

    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 1 clove garlic, minced
    • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
    • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

    For the toppings:

    • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • Freshly chopped basil (optional)

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Step: Make the pasta: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Create a well in the center and add the eggs and olive oil. Using a fork, gradually incorporate the flour into the wet ingredients until a shaggy dough forms.
  2. Step: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 5-10 minutes, or until smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour, 1 tablespoon at a time. If the dough is too dry, add a little more water, 1 teaspoon at a time.
  3. Step: Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let rest for 30 minutes at room temperature.
  4. Step: Divide the dough into four equal pieces. Working with one piece at a time, flatten it with your hands or a rolling pin into a thin sheet, about 1/16-inch thick.
  5. Step: Cut the pasta into desired shapes, such as tagliatelle, fettuccine, or pappardelle.
  6. Step: Make the sauce: Heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds, or until fragrant.
  7. Step: Add the crushed tomatoes, oregano, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook for 15-20 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly.
  8. Step: Cook the pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until al dente.
  9. Step: Drain the pasta and add it to the sauce. Toss to coat.
  10. Step: Serve the pasta immediately, topped with Parmesan cheese and fresh basil, if desired.
